Monster Energy Juiced Aussie Style Lemonade (Review)

If you're like anything like me, the very first thing you do after buying a new Monster is check the text on the back, there's always a short paragraph describing what inspired the creation of the flavour. But on Aussie Style Lemonade, it's strangely secretive about what this is supposed to be. It describes "Fresh citrus flavour"; but never goes into detail about what citrus fruit specifically. The ingredients list doesn't help either. My best guess is that the flavour is supposed to imitate grape fruit and lime. Both being fruits that I don't personally like. It's tart, but not in a particularly good way. Before I even put my lips to the can, it already tastes like I've bitten into a lime that's 2 months from being ripe. I think I might just be missing a certain taste gene or something, Because I know people who go crazy for this drink, But for me, It's just not there. Or maybe, like with many things in life, it's an acquired taste? It's a reminder that for as how much I can describe what something tastes like, how "good" it is, that will always be subjective. So while for me, this drink will be hard to finish, you might just adore it. "When life gives you lemons, throw them in the ocean"

3/10

Monster Energy Lewis Hamilton (Review)

It's no secret that I really like Monster Energy, and every time I get the chance to buy a new flavour that I haven't tasted yet, I jump on it. Despite this, my encyclopedia of monster tastes is small compared to the actual library of flavours out there. My research is limited by the decisions of local shop owners around my town to feature any given monster flavour. Regardless, I am confident that this has to be the first Monster ever created that is based of an actual living person. I'm not sure why Monster had to go and give that title to a racecar driver of all people, But at least the one they picked was in addition to a car racer, a handsome vegan, philanthropist. It's just a shame that unlike the career of Mr. Sir Lewis Hamilton, himself; The flavour of this Monster is very lacking. If I had to compare this flavour to any other Monster I've reviewed so far; It would no doubt have to be Ultra Watermelon. It being sugarless works against it, because the fruit flavour they were going for (That would be Peach and Nectarine, btw) was not enough to save the drink by itself. I've always thought making drinks like this sugarless is just a waste of time, It never tastes as good as it could if they had just used traditional sweeteners. I can see why someone would want a sugar-free drink. But if it's going to taste this boring, like, what's the point, y'know? in most cases Monster manages to pull their flavours into greatness even when they are sugarless. That's a thing I've praised them for in the past. But Lewis Hamilton is a rare instance of failure. If you're a fan of Peach and Nectarine, You might still like this. Taste is obviously subjective. Lastly I would like to say something nice about Monster Energy Lewis Hamilton, for once. I enjoy the design on the can. The pattern is obviously inspired by modern sportswear and it's very well executed. the stickers on the left side are great too. That's all I have to say, though. so let's finish of with a score of

4/10

Monster Ultra Fiesta Mango (Review)

Clearly, Someone at Monster didn't get the memo that they already had a Mango flavoured drink themed after Mexican festivals. You don't need to even drink it to tell that it's going to be similar to Monster Energy Juiced Mango Loco. And it is. The most noticeable different between the two is that Mango Loco, and other Juiced monsters, As I understand it, are made from real fruits. Where as Ultra is just artificial flavours blended together. This of course gives the Juiced Monsters an extra edge in the race. Although, I reckon they're probably more expensive to produce, too. I wouldn't go so far to say that Mango loco is a loss leader. But with the variety of different fruits they put in that thing, there price tag doesn't look so unreasonable anymore. Ultra fiesta on the other hand. It's more like they poured mango perfume into a vat of unflavoured energy drink. It doesn't taste bad. Not by any stretch of the imagination. But if I may be so bold as to compare it to a drink I last had almost a whole month ago. It's generally less rich in flavour. I find that It's hard to come up with a good reason to buy this monster. When Mango Loco is basically the same thing but better. The price is the same. And in my experience. Mango Loco is sold in more stores. So the Million dollar question is; Why does Monster Ultra Fiesta mango even exist? The only reason I can see for someone buying this, Is if they try to avoid Sugary drinks. Which is a valid excuse. But no other Juiced Monster has their own sugar-free version in Ultra, so why? It's strange. Ultra Fiesta Mango tastes good. But I can't think of a good reason why I would recommend it to someone. I know I sound like a broken record. But it's basically just a worse version of something that already exists. If you want mango, just buy Mango Loco.

5/10

Monster Energy JUICED Mango Loco (Review)

The ingredients are: Mango, Guava, Apple, Grape, Pineapple, Apricot, Peach, Orange, Passion fruit. with tropical ingredients like this it would be easy for it to fall right in line with the other millions of exotic drinks out there. but surprisingly. It doesn't taste like any other drink I know of. If i had to pin it down. I'd say that it's mostly thanks to the mango and guava. they stand out the most in the flavour. compared to other Monsters. this almost feels like it's just a regular drink. it's not highly carbonated or sour like other energy drinks. which I guess I should have expected from it being a Juice-based drink. I don't know if the caffine percentage is different in the "JUICED" line, but my heart doesn't beat nearly as rapidly when this is my energy drink of choice. maybe that's just a trick of the flavour, though. Anyhow. this is a good drink. and you can get it at pretty much every store you find regular Monster energy, so I'd say I recommend this.

7/10

Monster Energy Juiced Monarch (Review)

When the uninitiated hear Monster they imagine that tart super chemical-y flavour that you so often get with most energy drinks. But us scholars who study and appreciate these carefully crafted beverages see something very different In Monster Energy. Monster is not just drinks that make you feel awake in the morning or give you heart palpitations at 2am. It's a rich and beautiful library of the most delicious tastes ever known to men. No other Monster encapsulates this quite as well as Monarch. It's not my favourite one, not by a long shot. But it sets a standard for what a good drink really is. Those of you readers who think Monster Energy is just another over-hyped energy drink I urge you to try Monster Energy Juiced Monarch. It's the perfect drink for a spring afternoon, It makes my heart feel warm, even though I'm dead inside. It's all around a pretty good monster.

7/10, Tastes just like real butterflies!
